Home Town Hidden Object

Privacy statement

This statement describes how information may be handled when you use the mobile game distributed on the Apple App Store. It is written for players and families in clear, everyday language.

Scope

These notes apply only to the App Store build of Home Town Hidden Object running on your Apple devices. They do not describe other products or services.

Collection in the app

According to the App Privacy details shown on the product page at the time of this writing, the developer indicates that this app does not collect data from you through the game itself. If Apple’s label changes after an update, the App Store “App Privacy” section for this title should be treated as the up-to-date summary.

Data on your device

Like most games, progress, preferences, and similar play state may be stored locally so sessions can continue where you left off. You can remove that information by deleting the app or clearing its data using the settings on your device.

Apple and the platform

Installing, updating, or making in-app transactions goes through Apple’s systems. Those flows are governed by Apple’s own terms and privacy policies. Optional platform services such as analytics or crash reporting, where available, are managed through your device and Apple ID settings rather than a separate sign-in inside the game.

Advertising and age rating

The store listing indicates the experience may include advertising and is rated for ages four and older. Parents and guardians can use Screen Time and other Apple parental controls to manage installs, purchases, and permitted content on shared devices.

Changes

Gameplay or platform requirements can change over time. When they do, this overview may be revised so it still reflects how the product is meant to behave. The revision date at the bottom of the page is updated when the text changes in a meaningful way.

This page is a plain-language companion to the store listing. It does not grant legal rights beyond what applies in your country or region, and it does not replace Apple’s policies. For statutory privacy rights where they exist, use the privacy and data tools Apple provides for your Apple ID together with app management on your devices.
